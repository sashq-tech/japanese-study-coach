Japan Ready Coach

A beginner Japanese study coach I am building to rebuild my Japanese from the beginning and prepare for real life in Japan.

About Me

Hi, I'm Sean McDowell. I spent 24 years in the United States Air Force, and Japan gave me two of the best chapters of that career: six years near Yokota Air Base in western Tokyo from 1995 to 2001, and three more near Kadena Air Base on Okinawa from 2007 to 2010. My son was born in a local Okinawa hospital in 2008. Between those two tours I got to live the everyday version of Japan — trains, errands, trash days, and convenience-store runs — not the two-week vacation version.

I have not lived there since 2010, and I try to be honest about what that means: my instincts are real, but some of them are old. Over the years I spent plenty of money on Japanese books and apps to rebuild my language skills, and I always drifted away. The practice never felt personal or practical enough to stick with. So I built the study path I actually wanted to use — kana first, short sessions, and progress that stays on my own device.

My wife, Emi, is Japanese and visits family in Japan every year. She is this site's current-reality check. When my memory says one thing and modern Japan does another — how people pay, what small shops still expect, what has quietly changed — her perspective wins. That is also why I write practical notes like Okinawa vs Mainland Japan: I want the site to be clear about what I lived, what Emi keeps current, and what a beginner should still verify instead of trusting old assumptions.

Japan Ready Coach is the study coach I am using myself as Emi and I think seriously about life in Japan again. If it helps you get ready too — for a trip, a move, or a long-postponed goal — even better.

Purpose

This project is designed to help a beginner start gently with hiragana, katakana, and JLPT N5 basics before moving into harder material. The current app favors short practice loops, review, and daily continuity over long lessons.

What This App Covers

The current scope is kana-first N5 starter practice: recognition, vocabulary, particles, grammar patterns, sentence checks, and practical phrases. It is not a complete JLPT preparation product.

A Note on Accuracy

Japanese explanations and examples should be treated as learning aids that need review. The project does not guarantee language accuracy, naturalness, or official JLPT alignment.
